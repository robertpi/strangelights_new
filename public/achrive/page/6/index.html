Strings and F# Immutable Lists

In .NET strings are immutable. I think most .NET programmers agree that this is a good thing, as it generally makes code that works with strings safer and more predictable. However, operations that involve large amounts of string manipulation perform poorly because each time a string concatenation occurs then both strings must be copied. His often leads people to believe that all immutable objects will perform poorly. The aim of the article is to show that this is not the case, or at least it’s not as black and white as you may think. …
